Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces (340 grams) of spaghetti or fettuccine
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100 grams) grated Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan cheese)
  • 4 ounces (113 grams) of guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish, optional)

Prepare the Pasta:

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  • Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until it's al dente (usually 1-2 minutes less than the recommended time). Reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.

Cook the Guanciale or Pancetta:

  • While the pasta is cooking, heat a skillet over medium heat
  • Add the diced guanciale or pancetta to the skillet and cook until it's crispy and golden brown, about 4-5 minutes. If you're using pancetta, you might need to add a bit of olive oil to the pan.

Prepare the Sauce:

  • In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated Pecorino Romano cheese until well combined. Season with a generous amount of black pepper (about 1/2 to 1 teaspoon), and be mindful of the salt as the cheese is already salty.

Combine Pasta, Sauce, and Guanciale:

  • Quickly transfer the drained pasta to the skillet with the cooked guanciale or pancetta (off heat).
  • Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing everything together quickly and vigorously. The heat from the pasta will help create a creamy sauce as the eggs cook. If the sauce seems too thick, you can add a bit of the reserved pasta cooking water to thin it out.
